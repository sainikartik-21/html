Biography: Nikola Tesla

Name

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist. He is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.

Early Life

Tesla was born on 10 July 1856 in Smiljan, Croatia (then part of the Austrian Empire). He showed exceptional intelligence and inventiveness from a young age, which led him to pursue engineering and physics.

Achievements

Tesla developed the AC electrical system, including the AC induction motor. His inventions laid the foundation for wireless communication and power transmission. Tesla’s visionary ideas include concepts like the wireless transmission of energy, which are still being explored today. He held over 300 patents worldwide.
